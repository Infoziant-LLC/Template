Artificial Intelligence for Robotics

$89.00 FREE

Objective

Course Objectives This course aims to equip learners with a comprehensive understanding of how artificial intelligence (AI) enhances robotics. Participants will explore AI techniques for perception, decision-making, and control in robotic systems. The course will cover the integration of machine learning algorithms to improve robotic functionalities and adaptiveness. Students will gain hands-on experience with AI-driven robotics applications and their practical implementations. By the end, learners will be able to design and develop intelligent robotic systems that can perform complex tasks autonomously.

Course Outline

This module introduces the fundamental concepts of AI and its role in robotics. It covers the basics of AI algorithms, the importance of robotics in modern technology, and the overview of how AI enhances robotic systems. Learners will also explore the history and evolution of AI in robotics.
Focuses on how machine learning algorithms enable robots to interpret sensory data and understand their environment. Topics include image and speech recognition, sensor fusion, and data preprocessing techniques. Hands-on exercises will involve training models for object detection and recognition.
Covers decision-making algorithms used in robotics, including reinforcement learning, planning, and optimization techniques. The module will delve into how robots make decisions based on their environment and goals, and how these decisions are implemented in real-time scenarios.
Explores how AI integrates with robotic control systems to enhance their functionality. Topics include control theory, adaptive control, and the application of AI in controlling robot movements and actions. Practical sessions will involve programming and simulating robotic control systems.
Discusses emerging trends and advanced topics in AI for robotics, such as autonomous systems, multi-robot coordination, and ethical considerations. The module includes case studies of cutting-edge robotic applications and future directions for research and development in AI-driven robotics.
